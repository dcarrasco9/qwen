# Rocket Lab USA, Inc. (RKLB)

**Sector:** Industrials / Aerospace & Defense
**Exchange:** NASDAQ
**Headquarters:** Long Beach, California
**Founded:** 2006 by Peter Beck (New Zealand)

---

## Company Overview

Rocket Lab is an end-to-end space company providing launch services and space systems solutions. The company designs and manufactures rockets, spacecraft, and components, offering a vertically integrated approach from satellite design through launch and on-orbit operations.

### Business Segments

| Segment | Q3 2025 Revenue | Description |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Space Systems** | $114.2M (74%) | Spacecraft engineering, components, manufacturing, on-orbit operations |
| **Launch Services** | $40.8M (26%) | Dedicated and rideshare launches via Electron rocket |

## Technology Portfolio

### Electron Rocket (Operational)
- **Payload Capacity:** Up to 300 kg to LEO
- **Status:** 79 successful missions, 100% success rate in 2025
- **Engine:** Rutherford - first 3D-printed, battery-powered engine to reach orbit
- **Structure:** First orbital rocket made entirely from carbon composite
- **Reusability:** Partially reusable (helicopter mid-air capture recovery)
- **2025 Performance:** 21 launches (record cadence, up from 16 in 2024)

### Neutron Rocket (In Development)
- **Payload Capacity:** Up to 13,000 kg to LEO
- **Target Markets:** Mega-constellation deployment, interplanetary missions, human spaceflight
- **Reusability:** Fully reusable first stage with integrated fairing recovery
- **Engine:** Archimedes (3D-printed, LOX/methane)
- **Structure:** Carbon composite construction
- **First Launch:** Targeted Q1-Q2 2026 (delayed from original 2024 target)
- **Development Cost:** ~$360M through end of 2025

### Spacecraft Components & Capabilities
- Solar panels, reaction wheels, star trackers
- Separation systems, radios, flight software
- **Photon Platform:** Configurable satellite bus
- **Geost Acquisition** ($325M, 2025): Electro-optical and infrared sensors

## Competitive Position

### Strategic Moat
Rocket Lab is the **only proven commercial launch alternative to SpaceX** in the United States. This creates a "strategic premium" as the U.S. government and commercial customers require supply chain diversification.

### Unique Advantages

1. **Vertical Integration:** Controls entire value chain from components to launch to on-orbit operations
2. **Flight Heritage:** 79 successful missions with 100% success rate in 2025
3. **Launch Cadence:** Second most active launcher in the U.S.
4. **Defense Positioning:** Only publicly traded company with this capability and government trust
5. **Full-Stack Offering:** Unique ability to build spacecraft AND launch them

### Key Customers
- U.S. Space Development Agency (SDA)
- U.S. Space Force
- NASA
- Commercial constellation operators
- International government agencies

## Competition

### Primary Competitors

| Company | Vehicle | Payload | Status | Threat Level |
|---------|---------|---------|--------|--------------|
| **SpaceX** | Falcon 9 | 17,500 kg | Dominant | High |
| **Blue Origin** | New Glenn | 45,000 kg | Operational (2025) | High |
| **Relativity Space** | Terran R | 23,500 kg | Late 2026 target | Medium |
| **Firefly** | MLV | Medium-lift | In development | Medium |
| **ULA** | Vulcan | 27,200 kg | Operational | Medium |

### SpaceX Dominance
- Falcon 9 offers lower per-kilogram costs (~$67-70M per dedicated launch)
- SpaceX can theoretically drop prices to near-zero margins to starve competitors
- Controls ~90% of commercial launch market

### Blue Origin Threat
- New Glenn orbital debut in January 2025 (before Neutron)
- Heavy-lift capability directly competes for medium-to-heavy payloads
- Could consolidate non-SpaceX market before Neutron arrives

## Risks & Challenges

### Neutron Execution Risk
- Timeline pushed back multiple times (2024 → 2025 → 2026)
- First launch now expected no earlier than mid-2026
- Delays add ~$15M per quarter in costs
- Success is existential for long-term growth thesis

### Electron Limitations
- Physically limited to ~300 kg payloads
- Cannot serve heavy mega-constellation market (Kuiper, Starlink)
- Cannot compete for heavy national security missions
- Caps total addressable market for current vehicle

### Financial Risks
- Continued cash burn (~$220M LTM negative FCF)
- Heavy CAPEX for Neutron development
- Potential for dilutive equity raises
- Profitability not expected until 2027-2028

### Valuation Risk
- Trading at premium multiples (~15x+ forward revenue)
- Stock up 500%+ over two years
- Priced for near-perfect execution on Neutron

### Competitive Timing
- Blue Origin's New Glenn operational before Neutron
- Relativity's Terran R targeting similar timeline
- Market may consolidate before Neutron scales
