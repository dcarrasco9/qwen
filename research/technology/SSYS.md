# Stratasys Ltd. (SSYS)

**Sector:** Technology / Computer Hardware / 3D Printing
**Exchange:** NASDAQ
**Headquarters:** Eden Prairie, Minnesota (also Israel operations)
**Founded:** 1989 by S. Scott Crump and Lisa Crump
**Employees:** ~1,779

---

## Company Overview

Stratasys is a global leader in polymer-based 3D printing and additive manufacturing solutions. The company provides industrial-grade 3D printers, materials, software, and on-demand parts for industries including aerospace, automotive, healthcare, consumer products, and defense.

### Core Business Segments
- **3D Printing Systems**: Industrial and commercial printers
- **Consumables**: Proprietary materials and filaments
- **Software**: GrabCAD and manufacturing workflow tools
- **Parts Services**: On-demand 3D printed parts (Stratasys Direct)

---

## Technology Portfolio

### Five Core Technologies

| Technology | Description | Use Cases |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| **FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling)** | Extrusion-based thermoplastic process | Functional prototyping, tooling, production parts |
| **PolyJet** | Multi-material jetting with vivid colors/textures | Concept models, prototypes with realistic finish |
| **P3 (DLP)** | Advanced digital light processing | Small-batch production, high accuracy parts |
| **SAF (Selective Absorption Fusion)** | Powder bed fusion with thermoplastics | High-volume production |
| **Stereolithography** | Resin-based photopolymerization | High-detail prototypes, patterns |

### Software
- **GrabCAD**: Industry-leading manufacturing workflow software
- Enables seamless design-to-print experience across hardware platforms

### Recent Technology Expansion
- **Metal 3D Printing** (November 2025): Partnership with Tritone Technologies
  - Adds production-grade metal and ceramic capabilities
  - Expands beyond polymer-only offerings

## Risks & Challenges

### Macroeconomic Headwinds
- Constrained global demand for AM solutions
- Inflationary pressures on costs
- Cautious corporate capital spending
- Supply chain bottlenecks

### Tariff Impact
- Margins significantly impacted by tariffs
- Price increases implemented but slow to offset costs
- Recovery expected to take multiple quarters

### Competition
- Intense competition from low-cost Chinese manufacturers
- Desktop segment faces falling prices and margin pressure
- Ultimaker investment impaired due to market conditions

### Financial Performance Trends
- Revenue declining (-6% YoY TTM, -8.79% in 2024)
- Gross margins under pressure (41% vs 44.8% prior year)
- Net losses widening on non-cash impairments
- Cyclical nature of capital equipment spending

### Corporate History
- Desktop Metal merger terminated (September 2023) after shareholder rejection
- Strategic direction questions following failed M&A
